# Configure Custom Subdomain

## Introduction

Setting up a custom subdomain for your project enhances its branding and accessibility. Follow these steps to configure a custom subdomain:

## Step 1: Create a New Project

Start by creating a new project in your dashboard.

- Click on "New Project" to initiate the project creation process.
- Enter a **Project Name**: Ensure it is alphanumeric and does not contain spaces.
- Optionally, enter a **Project Subdomain**: This will be part of your project's URL (e.g., `subdomain.example.com`).

## Step 2: Navigate to Project Settings

Once your project is created, navigate to the project settings page.

<Steps>
### Step 1

Click on the "Settings" or "Project Settings" option for your specific project.

</Steps>

## Step 3: Add Subdomain

In the project settings, find the option to add or configure subdomains for your project.

<Steps>
### Step 1

Enter your desired subdomain name (e.g., `subdomain.example.com`) in the provided input field.

<Image src="/images/subdomain.png" alt="Subdomain Setup" width={500} height={500} />
</Steps>

Click on the confirm button. You will recive a green snackbar on success.

## Step 4: Verify and Save

Follow any verification steps prompted by the platform to confirm ownership of the subdomain.

<Steps>
### Step 3

Verify domain ownership by adding necessary DNS records or following platform-specific instructions.

</Steps>

## Step 5: Confirm Setup

Once verified, save the changes to apply the subdomain configuration to your project.

<Callout>
	Setting up a custom subdomain allows you to personalize your project's URL and
	enhance its visibility.
</Callout>

## Conclusion

By following these steps, you can successfully configure a custom subdomain for your project, ensuring easier access and better branding for your users.
